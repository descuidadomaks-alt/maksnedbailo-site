You've got a great product. Great reviews. A solid reputation. And yet — leads keep disappearing before they ever become customers.

Most owners assume it's a marketing problem. More ads, better copy, a new landing page. But the real leak is happening *after* the lead arrives.

## The Number You Need to Know

**Harvard Business Review found that businesses that respond to leads within the first hour are 7x more likely to qualify that lead** than those who respond even an hour later. After 24 hours, the odds drop off a cliff.

The average small business responds to an inbound enquiry in **19 hours**.

Let that sink in.

## What's Actually Happening in Your Customer's Mind

When someone messages you, they're in a moment of intent. They've decided they want what you offer. They've pulled out their phone, typed out a message, and hit send.

And then they wait.

Here's what happens during that wait:
- They message your competitor
- They find a YouTube video that answers their question (and now they're not sure they need you)
- Life gets in the way — the moment of intent passes
- They feel like they're not a priority

By the time you reply, the sale is already lost. Not because you did anything wrong. Because someone else was faster.

## The 85% Statistic That Should Terrify You

**85% of customers who can't reach a business on the first try will never try again.** They don't write a complaint. They don't leave a bad review. They just silently dial your competitor.

This is the most expensive thing happening in your business right now — and it's completely invisible unless you're looking for it.

## What "Fast Enough" Actually Looks Like in 2026

Five years ago, responding within 24 hours was considered professional. Today, customers expect a response in minutes.

Not because they're impatient — because they've been conditioned by Amazon, Uber, and every other service that responds instantly. Their baseline has shifted.

If you're not responding in under 5 minutes, you're fighting an uphill battle.

## The Fix Isn't Hiring More Staff

The gut reaction is to think: "I need to hire someone to answer messages." But this creates a new problem — a person who costs €2,000–4,000/month, takes sick days, needs training, and can only work certain hours.

The actual solution is to make your business respond *automatically* — immediately, in any language, 24/7 — while you're with a client, at dinner, or finally sleeping.

This is what smart assistants do. Not to replace the human conversation, but to *start* it. To capture the lead, answer the common questions, and flag the important ones for you to follow up on.

## What to Do This Week

1. **Test your own response time.** Message yourself from a friend's phone. How long does it take to reply? Be honest.
2. **Map your leaks.** Which channels are you slowest on — WhatsApp, website contact form, Instagram DMs?
3. **Start with one channel.** You don't need to automate everything at once. Pick the highest-volume channel and fix that first.
